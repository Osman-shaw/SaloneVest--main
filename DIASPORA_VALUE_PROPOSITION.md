## 3. PROBLEM TO SOLVE

### The Core Problem:
**"The diaspora is eager to invest in Sierra Leone's future, but they are blocked by high fees, currency risk, and a lack of trusted, vetted opportunities. Our best youth-led businesses are starved of capital as a result."**

### Breakdown of the Problem:

#### A. **For Diaspora Investors:**

**Problem 1: Prohibitive Remittance Fees (7-10%)**
- Traditional money transfer: Western Union, MoneyGram charge 7-10%
- Example: Sending $1,000 costs $70-100 in fees alone
- Bank transfers: 3-5% plus slow processing (3-7 days)
- **Impact:** A $500/month diaspora investor loses $3,600/year to fees

**Problem 2: Currency & Exchange Risk**
- Sierra Leone Leone (SLL) highly volatile
- Official rate vs. street rate discrepancies (5-15%)
- Inflation eroding purchasing power (20-30% annually)
- **Impact:** $10,000 investment loses $2,000-3,000 in currency devaluation
- No hedge against local currency risk

**Problem 3: Trust & Verification Challenges**
- No centralized, vetted investment database
- Hard to distinguish legitimate opportunities from scams
- No regulatory oversight of local investment schemes
- Family/friend connections vulnerable to fraud
- **Impact:** Diaspora investors paralyzed by fear; billions sit idle

**Problem 4: Access to Opportunities**
- Startups don't know how to reach diaspora capital
- No formal fundraising infrastructure
- LinkedIn/informal networks are unreliable
- Geographic distance creates information gap
- **Impact:** Talented entrepreneurs can't access growth capital

**Problem 5: Portfolio Management & Transparency**
- No way to track multiple investments across different people
- Receive updates via WhatsApp/email (unreliable)
- No real-time performance tracking
- Tax documentation scattered across sources
- **Impact:** Investors lose track; difficult to optimize portfolio

#### B. **For Sierra Leone Entrepreneurs & Businesses:**

**Problem 1: Capital Starvation**
- Limited domestic capital (few banks, high interest rates: 20-35%)
- Government bonds inaccessible to small businesses
- Venture capital ecosystem nascent (few local VCs)
- **Impact:** High-potential startups fail for lack of $10K-$100K seed

**Problem 2: Expensive Debt**
- Commercial bank loans: 25-35% interest rates
- Microfinance: 30-50% rates with strict collateral requirements
- Informal lending: 50%+ with predatory terms
- No equity financing options
- **Impact:** Businesses can't afford to grow; profits consumed by interest

**Problem 3: Diaspora Capital is Inaccessible**
- Diaspora wants to invest but can't find opportunities
- Traditional investment structures require physical presence
- Legal/regulatory barriers to diaspora investment
- No trusted intermediary
- **Impact:** $50-100M in annual diaspora capital unused

**Problem 4: Limited Investment Types**
- No mutual funds accessible to diaspora
- No government bond/treasury access for diaspora
- No diversified portfolio options
- All-or-nothing bet on single opportunities
- **Impact:** Diaspora forced into high-risk or no investment

#### C. **For Sierra Leone Economy:**
- **GDP Growth**: Hampered by lack of capital for productive sectors
- **Job Creation**: Youth unemployment at 40%+; businesses can't hire without growth capital
- **Financial Inclusion**: Only 20% of population has bank accounts
- **Technology Adoption**: Digital solutions can't scale without venture funding

### The Paradox:
- **Supply Side:** Diaspora has $50-100M annually to invest
- **Demand Side:** Sierra Leone has $500M+ in capital needs
- **Result:** Massive inefficiency; both sides lose
